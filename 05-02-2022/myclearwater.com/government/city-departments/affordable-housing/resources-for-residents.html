NEW: Important Information for Residents Behind on Their Mortgage Due to COVID

From the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development:

As the impacts from the COVID-19 pandemic continue to ripple across our nation, an escalating foreclosure crisis among FHA-insured borrowers is reaching a cataclysmic point impacting hundreds of thousands of borrowers. We understand that many homeowners may be struggling and unable to make mortgage payments due to the COVID-19 pandemic.

If you or someone you know are one of the many FHA-borrowers in communities across the country having difficulty paying your mortgage due to the pandemic, we urge you to act now. For more information on how to obtain assistance click here.

Down Payment Assistance

Down Payment Assistance Program

Loan funds are available to help homebuyers cover part of their down payment and closing costs when purchasing a home. The Down Payment Assistance Program is funded through the federal HOME Investment Partnership (HOME Program) and the State Housing Initiatives Partnership (SHIP) Program.

The city of Clearwater, through its approved non-profit housing agencies, provides down payment and closing costs assistance to income eligible households who meet program criteria. Assistance is provided in the form of a loan, 50% of which will be amortized over 20 years, payment on the other 50% will be deferred and forgiven at loan maturity, so long as no event of default exists.  If the borrower defaults on any of the terms of the mortgage, the deferred amount will become due and payable. If the home is sold prior to the maturity date, the entire outstanding balance will be due to the City. 

Payments will begin on the 50% amortized balance after a five-year deferral period. The property to be purchased must be the borrower’s principal residence and must be located within the city limits of Clearwater. The maximum loan amount is $45,000. The maximum sales price is $244,000 for an existing home and $263,000 for a newly constructed home.

Requirements for the Down Payment Assistance Program:

  • Borrowers/Participants must meet program income guidelines. 
  • Homes must be located within the city limits of the city of Clearwater. 
  • Borrowers/Participants must provide documentation of their household income and assets along with other program requirements.
  • Approval from a first mortgage lender must be obtained.
  • Completion of a Homebuyer Education Program through a HUD-approved housing counseling agency, and receipt of certificate for class completion.
  • Homebuyers must contribute at least 1% of the sales/purchase price toward the closing from their own funds.
  • Funding for those at 120% AMI is limited and available on a first-come, first-served basis.

Homeowner Rehabilitation Program

Homeowner Rehabilitation Program

The city of Clearwater’s Homeowner Rehabilitation Program is designed to help maintain decent, safe, and affordable housing for Clearwater residents. The program helps homeowners to bring their homes into compliance with the City’s minimum Housing Code and the Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) quality standards.

To be eligible for the city of Clearwater’s Homeowner Rehabilitation Program, the property must be your primary residence and located within the city limits of Clearwater. The property must not be subject to overdue mortgage payments, property taxes or judgments of any amount. Property insurance (and flood insurance, if required) must be current and remain in effect for the entire loan term. A maximum of two mortgages is allowed. 

The applicant’s total current combined gross annual household income from all sources cannot exceed the limits set by HUD for Tampa-St Petersburg-Clearwater, Florida Metropolitan Statistical Area (MSA).

Income Eligibility Charts

The property must be one of the following:

  • Single-family residence
  • Villa
  • Townhome

Rehabilitation plans must address all code related repairs found in the home.

Eligible Repairs

Priority is given to the following repairs:

  • Roof
  • Plumbing
  • HVAC
  • Electric
  • ADA Modifications of an Immediate Nature

Ineligible Repairs

The Homeowner Rehabilitation Program may not be used to provide luxury items or any remodeling purely cosmetic in nature. Kitchen upgrades, swimming pools, hot tubs or decorative items will not be considered. All non-health or non-safety related remodeling requests will not be considered. Homes with mold damage and structural issues are ineligible for this program.

The assistance will be in the form of a deferred payment or low interest loan, depending on qualifications. A deferred payment loan allows the borrower to delay repayment on the loan as long as they reside in the home with no default in the terms. 

Priority will be given to persons with special needs as defined in s.420.0004, Florida Statutes, and elderly households that need renovations to allow individuals to remain independent in their homes and prevent having to move into an assisted living type arrangement.

 

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Q: What is the city of Clearwater’s Housing Rehabilitation Program?
A: The city of Clearwater’s Housing Rehabilitation Program is designed to primarily correct code-related deficiencies found within the home.

Q: Who is Eligible?
A: You are eligible if you meet the requirements listed below:

  1. Income requirements.
  2. Your property is located within the city limits of Clearwater.
  3. The property is your homestead property.
  4. Your mortgage payment is current.
  5. There are no outstanding liens or judgments against the property.
  6. You have current homeowners insurance and flood insurance if your property is located within a flood zone.

Q: What can I have fixed?
A: Property owners can have those items fixed that are noted by a property inspector to not meet the city’s Minimum Housing Code, such as a leaking or failed roof system.

Q: Do I have to pay back the funds?
A: All funds will have to be paid back. If your household is determined to be a very low-income household, the funds will be in the form of a deferred payment with no interest and/or payment as long as you reside in the home as a principal residence, and do not sell, rent, refinance or transfer title.

Q: How do I apply?
A: Interested persons can apply by contacting the Housing Division of the Economic Development and Housing Department at 727-562-4030.

Q: How long does the process take?
A: Depending on the extent of the repairs and improvements to be made, the average process will take about six months to complete from application to completion.

Q: Can I have handicap modifications done to my home?
A: Yes. Funding is made available for owner-occupied rehabilitation assistance to serve persons with special needs as defined in section 420.004 Florida Statutes. These special needs funds must be to serve persons with developmental disabilities as defined in section 393.063, Florida Statutes, with an emphasis on home modifications, including technological enhancements and devices, which will allow homeowners to remain independent in their homes and maintain their homeownership.

Q: Are mobile homes eligible?
A: No. The city does not assist with repairs of mobile homes.

Q: What happens if I sell my home?
A: If you sell your home, or rent, refinance, transfer title or if the home ceases to be your principal residence, the city’s loan will become due and payable.

Q: What is the maximum loan that I can get?
A: The maximum assistance is $45,000.

For more information on the Homeowner Rehabilitation Program, please contact Terry Malcolm-Smith at (727) 562-4036.

Frequently Asked Questions

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Below is a list of the most commonly asked questions at the City of Clearwater with guidance on finding answers.

Q: I (and/or my family) am homeless or at risk of becoming homeless. Where can I find help?

A: Click here for available resources. Call 211 for assistance. 

 

Q: I am being treated unfairly by my landlord (e.g. facing wrongful eviction, poor living conditions, etc.). Where can I find help? 

A: Contact Gulf Cast Legal Services. Depending on qualifications, they may be able to provide low-cost or free legal services. https://gulfcoastlegal.org/

 

Q: I want to purchase a home in Clearwater, but I need professional guidance and financial assistance. Where can I find help? 

A: Click here for financial assistance and information on locating a HUD-approved Homebuyer Education Counselor. 

 

Q: Where can I find an affordable apartment to rent? 

A: Please see the Affordable Housing Options section of our website for more information: Affordable Housing Options - Pinellas County

 

Q: I am pregnant (or a new mom) and need housing and other resources. Where can I find help? 

A: Click here for contact information for the Kimberly Home Pregnancy Resource Center. 

 

Q: Where can I find financial assistance for housing and utility payments for a hardship related to the pandemic? 

A: Pinellas County rent/utility assistance: Pinellas County Rent/Utility Assistance Program; Clearwater Mortgage Assistance program: Clearwater Emergency Mortgage Assistance Program

 

Q: How can I help a homeless person, generally help the homeless population, or provide volunteer services? 

A: Click here for information on how to best assist our homeless residents.

 

Q: How do I know if I income qualify for assistance under the City’s programs? 

A: Most of the City’s funding is reserved for households with total household income at or below 80% of Area Median Income (AMI). A small portion of funding is available for households earning up to 120% AMI. Click here for current income limits. 

 

Q: What types of public services are available in Clearwater? 

A: Click here and choose Public Services Available to Residents for a list of agencies and organizations that provide services to Clearwater residents though a partnership with the City of Clearwater.

 

Q: I am a homeowner in Clearwater and my home needs repairs. How can I find assistance?

A: Click here and choose Homeowner Rehabilitation Program for information on the City’s Home Rehabilitation program. 

 

Q: I have a Clearwater address. How do I know if I live within the incorporated limits of Clearwater? 

A: Your residence MUST be located in the CW, CWD, or CWDO Tax District. You can verify your tax district on the Pinellas County Property Appraiser’s website. Click here (Property Appraiser Search) and select “Search Our Database” then select “By Address” and enter your address. If you see a Tax District that is not CW, CWD, or CWDO you do not qualify for our programs.
