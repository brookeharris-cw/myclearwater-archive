Public Hearing - Ordinance No. 9575-22 - July 21 & Aug. 4

CITY OF CLEARWATER PUBLIC HEARING NOTICE

LAND USE PLAN AND ZONING ATLAS AMENDMENTS

NOTE: All persons wishing to address an item need to be present at the BEGINNING of the Community Development Board meeting.  Those cases that are not contested by the applicant, staff, neighboring property owners, etc. will be placed on a consent agenda and approved by a single vote at the beginning of the meeting.

The City of Clearwater, Florida, proposes to adopt the following ordinance:

ORDINANCE NO.  9575-22

AN ORDINANCE OF THE CITY OF CLEARWATER, FLORIDA, AMENDING THE FUTURE LAND USE ELEMENT OF THE COMPREHENSIVE PLAN OF THE CITY, TO CHANGE THE LAND USE DESIGNATION FOR CERTAIN REAL PROPERTY LOCATED ON THE EAST SIDE OF SOUTH HIGHLAND AVENUE APPROXIMATELY 450 FEET SOUTH OF DRUID ROAD, WHOSE POST OFFICE ADDRESS IS 905 SOUTH HIGHLAND AVENUE, CLEARWATER, FLORIDA 33756, FROM RESIDENTIAL/OFFICE GENERAL (R/OG), TO INSTITUTIONAL (I); PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE. 

Schedule of Public Hearings:

Tuesday, June 21, 2022 before the Community Development Board, at 1:00 p.m.

Thursday, July 21, 2022 before the City Council (1st Reading), at 6:00 p.m.

Thursday, August 4, 2022 before the City Council (2nd Reading), at 6:00 p.m.

 

All public hearings on the ordinance will be held in City Council Chambers, Clearwater Main Library, 100 N. Osceola Ave., Clearwater, Florida. (YMCA of the Suncoast Inc)  LUP2022-03003  Assigned Planner Dylan Prins (727) 562-4539

Interested parties may appear and be heard at the hearing or file written notice of approval or objection with the Planning and Development Director or City Clerk prior to the hearing.  Any person who decides to appeal any decision made by the Board or Council, with respect to any matter considered at such hearings, will need to request a record of the proceedings and, for such purpose, may need to ensure that a verbatim record of the proceedings is made, which record includes the testimony and evidence upon which the appeal is to be based per Florida Statute 286.0105.  Community Development Code Sec 4-206 states that party status shall be granted by the Board in quasi-judicial cases if the person requesting such status demonstrates that s/he is substantially affected.  Party status entitles parties to personally testify, present evidence, argument and witnesses, cross-examine witnesses, appeal the decision and speak on reconsideration requests, and needs to be requested and obtained during the case discussion before the CDB.

An oath will be administered swearing in all persons giving testimony in quasi-judicial public hearing cases.  If you wish to speak at the meeting, please wait to be recognized, then state and spell your name and provide your address.  Persons without party status speaking before the CDB shall be limited to three minutes unless an individual is representing a group in which case the Chairperson may authorize a reasonable amount of time up to 10 minutes.  The Community Development Board will review the case and make a recommendation to the City Council for final disposition.

Five days prior to the CDB meeting, staff reports and recommendations on the above requests will be available for review by interested parties between the hours of 8:30 a.m. and 4:30 p.m., weekdays, at the City of Clearwater, Planning and Development Department, 100 S. Myrtle Ave., Clearwater, FL 33756.  Please contact the Planning Department (727) 562-4567 to discuss any questions or concerns about the project and/or to better understand the proposal.

Gina Clayton                                                                                                                Rosemarie Call, MPA, MMC

Planning and Development Director                                                                                City Clerk

City of Clearwater

P.O. Box 4748, Clearwater, FL 33758-4748

Public Hearing - Resolution 22-06 approving a Development Agreement between the City of Clearwater and Beachrock Lodging LLLP - Aug. 4

CITY OF CLEARWATER

NOTICE OF INTENT TO CONSIDER

A DEVELOPMENT AGREEMENT

A public hearing will be conducted before the City Council on August 4, 2022, beginning at 6:00 PM (or as soon thereafter as the matter may be heard), in City Council Chambers, 1st Floor of the Main Library, 100 North Osceola Ave., Clearwater, Florida, to consider Resolution 22-06 approving a Development Agreement between the City of Clearwater and Beachrock Lodging LLLP, a Florida limited partnership (as assigned) for property located at 325 South Gulfview Boulevard and 326 Coronado Drive and legally described as Parcel Nos. 07-29-15-52380-000-0630 and 07-29-15-52380-000-1110. HDA2013-08004A. Assigned Planner: Mark Parry (727) 562-4741

Public Notice - Notice of Funding Availability SHIP Program FY 2021-2022

PUBLIC NOTICE
CITY OF CLEARWATER, FL
NOTICE OF FUNDING AVAILABILITY STATE HOUSING INITIATIVES PARTNERSHIP
(SHIP) PROGRAM FOR FY 2021-2022

The City of Clearwater anticipates receiving $781,532 for fiscal year 2021-2022 funding distributed by Florida Housing Finance Corporation through and operated under the State Housing Initiatives Partnership (SHIP) Program. Eligible strategies are down payment and closing costs assistance for newly constructed homes, down payment and closing cost assistance for existing homes, owner occupied rehabilitation, multi-family housing and other activities included in its Local Housing Assistance Plan. A copy of the Local Housing Assistance Plan is available from the contact person identified below or via the City’s website www.myclearwater.com/affordablehousing.

These funds will primarily be used to assist eligible homebuyers in the purchase of homes within the city limits of Clearwater. For current homeowners, rehabilitation assistance may be provided to assist with code deficiencies within their dwellings.

Applications for funded strategies will be made available between October 1, 2021 and June 30, 2022. Preference will be given to persons with special needs as defined in section 393.063, Florida Statutes.

Assistance is available to households whose maximum income is at or below 120% of Area Median Income (AMI).  Priority will be given to households at 80% or below Area Median Income (AMI).

For further information, contact Ms. Terry Malcolm-Smith at 727-562-4036 or via email at terry.malcolm-smith@myclearwater.com; or in person at the City of Clearwater Economic Development & Housing Department located at 600 Cleveland Street, Suite 600, Clearwater, between the hours of 8:00 a.m. through 5:00 p.m. Monday thru Friday.

Invitation to Bid - Directions for Living - July 27
INVITATION TO BID

Directions for Living is soliciting sealed bids from licensed contractors to provide the materials and labor for the following project at their Clearwater Center, 1437 South Belcher Road, Clearwater, FL 33764.  

Install one Double Record 8100 automatic swing door with two wave-to-open sensors for our front entry door and one Single Record 8100 automatic swing door with one wave-to-open sensor for our back parking lot entrance.

A Pre-Bid Meeting will be held onsite (at address listed above) on Wednesday, July 27, 2022 at 11:00 AM to review construction plans and specifications. Attendance at the Pre-Bid Meeting is strongly encouraged. Copies of the contract documents and bid requirements will be sent by email following the Pre-Bid Meeting. An active SAM.gov registration is required for a contract to be awarded.

All bids must include a determined explanation of cost and estimated timeline. Bids must be sealed and submitted to Directions for Living, c/o Chris Johnson, 1437 South Belcher Road, Clearwater, FL 33764 by Wednesday, August 3, 2022 at 3:00 PM, at which time all bids received will be publicly opened. Bids received after that time will not be considered. Please contact Chris Johnson, Directions for Living, at (727) 524-4464 ext. 1410 for additional project information. Directions for Living will award a firm fixed-price contract to the lowest responsive and responsible bidder and reserves the right to reject any and all bids received.  

This project will be utilizing Community Development Block Grant (CDBG) funds provided through the United States Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD). Therefore, Federal Regulations will apply including, but not limited to, the following: Federal Labor Standards per 29 CFR parts 3 & 5; Procurement Procedures per part 85 Uniform Administrative Requirements; Affirmative Action to achieve Equal Employment Opportunity per 41 CFR 60-4; and the DavisBacon Act.  Minority and Woman Owned businesses are encouraged to apply.
