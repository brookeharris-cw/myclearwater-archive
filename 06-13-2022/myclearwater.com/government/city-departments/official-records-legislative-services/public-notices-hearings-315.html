Public Notice - Proposed Program Year 2022 Annual Action Plan

CITY OF CLEARWATER
PROPOSED PROGRAM YEAR 2022 ANNUAL ACTION PLAN
30-DAY PUBLIC COMMENT PERIOD
PUBLIC HEARINGS

The City of Clearwater is hereby publishing the proposed Annual Action Plan (AAP) for Program Year (PY) 2022 to allocate federal funding from the Community Development Block Grant (CDBG) and HOME Investment Partnerships Program (HOME) under the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD). The AAP identifies projects and activities to be implemented by the City during PY 2022, which begins on October 1, 2022, and ends on September 30, 2023. In addition to the entitlement grant, funding allocations also include anticipated program income generated by the City’s Revolving Loan Fund as well as prior-year (i.e., carryover) resources from projects that were either cancelled or completed under budget to be applied toward new projects. CDBG funds are used to provide decent housing, suitable living environments, and economic opportunities to principally benefit low- and moderate-income persons. HOME funds are used to foster private sector partnerships with for-profit and non-profit organizations to develop affordable housing.

The City of Clearwater anticipates an estimated $897,475 in new CDBG funds, $1,231,980 in CDBG program income, and $767,422 in prior-year CDBG resources. The City also anticipates an estimated $509,528 in new HOME funds, $1,967,722 in HOME program income, and $2,003,239 in prior-year HOME resources.  These funds will be programmed to eligible activities under these grant programs.

The list below captures all proposed projects to be funded with CDBG and HOME resources in PY 2022. Funding for these and other projects are estimated resources only and funding amounts may increase or decrease based upon actual program income, prior-year adjustments, and/or final federal funding allocations. Due to these and other factors, the City of Clearwater reserves the right to add or delete projects and/or adjust funding allocations proportionally.

Public Hearings - Ordinance No. 9554-22 - June 2

CITY OF CLEARWATER PUBLIC HEARING NOTICE

LAND USE PLAN AND ZONING ATLAS AMENDMENTS

NOTE: All persons wishing to address an item need to be present at the BEGINNING of the Community Development Board meeting.  Those cases that are not contested by the applicant, staff, neighboring property owners, etc. will be placed on a consent agenda and approved by a single vote at the beginning of the meeting.

AN ORDINANCE OF THE CITY OF CLEARWATER, FLORIDA, AMENDING THE FUTURE LAND USE ELEMENT OF THE COMPREHENSIVE PLAN OF THE CITY, TO CHANGE THE LAND USE DESIGNATION FOR A PORTION OF CERTAIN REAL PROPERTY LOCATED ON THE NORTHEAST CORNER OF GULF TO BAY BOULEVARD AND SOUTH KEYSTONE AVENUE, WHOSE POST OFFICE ADDRESS IS 1640 GULF TO BAY BOULEVARD, CLEARWATER, FLORIDA 33755, FROM RESIDENTIAL/OFFICE GENERAL (R/OG), TO COMMERCIAL GENERAL (CG); PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.

Schedule of Public Hearings:

Tuesday, April 19, 2022 before the Community Development Board, at 1:00 p.m.

Thursday, May 19, 2022 before the City Council (1st Reading), at 6:00 p.m.

Thursday, June 2, 2022 before the City Council (2nd Reading), at 6:00 p.m.

 

All public hearings on the ordinance will be held in City Council Chambers, Clearwater Main Library, 100 N. Osceola Ave., Clearwater, Florida. (MSCW-GTB LLC)  LUP2022-01001  Assigned Planner Kyle Brotherton (727) 562-4626

Interested parties may appear and be heard at the hearing or file written notice of approval or objection with the Planning and Development Director or City Clerk prior to the hearing.  Any person who decides to appeal any decision made by the Board or Council, with respect to any matter considered at such hearings, will need to request a record of the proceedings and, for such purpose, may need to ensure that a verbatim record of the proceedings is made, which record includes the testimony and evidence upon which the appeal is to be based per Florida Statute 286.0105.  Community Development Code Sec 4-206 states that party status shall be granted by the Board in quasi-judicial cases if the person requesting such status demonstrates that s/he is substantially affected.  Party status entitles parties to personally testify, present evidence, argument and witnesses, cross-examine witnesses, appeal the decision and speak on reconsideration requests, and needs to be requested and obtained during the case discussion before the CDB.

An oath will be administered swearing in all persons giving testimony in quasi-judicial public hearing cases.  If you wish to speak at the meeting, please wait to be recognized, then state and spell your name and provide your address.  Persons without party status speaking before the CDB shall be limited to three minutes unless an individual is representing a group in which case the Chairperson may authorize a reasonable amount of time up to 10 minutes.  The Community Development Board will review the case and make a recommendation to the City Council for final disposition.

Five days prior to the CDB meeting, staff reports and recommendations on the above requests will be available for review by interested parties between the hours of 8:30 a.m. and 4:30 p.m., weekdays, at the City of Clearwater, Planning and Development Department, 100 S. Myrtle Ave., Clearwater, FL 33756.  Please contact the Planning Department (727) 562-4567 to discuss any questions or concerns about the project and/or to better understand the proposal.

Invitation to Bid - St. Vincent de Paul Community Kitchen and Resource Center - by May 25

INVITATION TO BID

St. Vincent de Paul Community Kitchen and Resource Center (SVDP) is soliciting sealed bids from licensed contractors to provide the materials and labor for the following scope of work at the project site at 1345 Park Street, Clearwater, FL 33756, and bids may be submitted for one or more of the following:

 

  1. Reception Office Renovation and Expansion
  • Demo existing front wall and frame new wall
  • Move Electrical as needed and install light fixtures
  • Supply and install tempered glass bi-pass window with channels & lock
  • Re-grid ceiling to new wall
  • Supply and install tile flooring and cove base
  • Supply and install four (4) new HDI (or equivalent) base cabinets for storage
  • Supply and install open shelving/bins between window systems
  • Supply and install plastic laminate countertops

     

    1. Bathroom Renovation
  • Remove and install new double sink and countertop
  • Remove and install new tile flooring and cove base
  • Remove and replace stall wall and two doors with locks
  • Patch and paint existing walls and entry door

     

    1. Kitchen Renovation
  • Replace Kitchen Line of Equipment: gas burner 36” range, gas double stack convection oven and manual 40-gallon tilt skillet
  • Installation and gas hookup of equipment
  • Replacement of stainless-steel prep tables

 

A mandatory pre-bid meeting will be held on Wednesday, May 18, 2022, at 1:00 PM in the Chapel at the project site. Prospective bidders are required to attend and sign in.  Copies of the bid requirements will be sent by email following the pre-bid meeting.

 

Bids must be sealed and submitted to SVDP, or may be emailed in pdf format, c/o Christine Bond, 1345 Park Street, Clearwater, FL 33756 or christine@svdpclearwaterfl.org by Wednesday, May 25, 2022, at 1:00 PM, at which time all bids received will be publicly opened and tabulated. Any bids received after that date and time will not be considered. Bids must include the following:

 

  • Contractor Information
    • Contractor background
    • Contract information
    • License
    • References – provide three references
  • Price – to include labor, materials, and permitting costs
  • Warranty
    • Manufacturer’s warranty
    • Contractor’s workmanship warranty
  • Timeline
  • Estimated timeline to complete the work

 

Bids will be evaluated based on the following criteria: a) quality of the material products being proposed for the job. b) the ability of the contractor to perform and complete the job in the proposed timeline. c) the overall cost of the job. d) SVDP will award a firm, fixed-price contract to the lowest responsive and responsible bidder, and SVPD reserves the right to reject any and all bids received. Please direct all questions regarding the project to the following: Christine Bond, Executive Director, at christine@svdpclearwaterfl.org.

 

This project will be utilizing Community Development Block Grant (CDBG) funds provided through the United States Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD). Therefore, Federal Regulations will apply, including, but not limited to, the following: Federal Labor Standards per 29 CFR parts 3 & 5; Procurement Procedures per part 85 Uniform Administrative Requirements; Affirmative Action to achieve Equal Employment Opportunity per 41 CFR 60-4; and the Davis-Bacon Act. Minority and Woman Owned businesses are encouraged to apply.
