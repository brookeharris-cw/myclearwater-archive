Call for Artists!

Submit your art in our contest, and it may be placed on a Clearwater city vehicle.

Contest Details

Children and teens under the age of 18 are invited to submit a scanned drawing, painting or graphic art using the form below.

Participants may draw, paint or color an image that is inspired by one of the following prompts:

“Keep the sea plastic-free”

“Save a critter, pick up litter”

“Toes in the sand, trash in the can”

“Our beach is not an ashtray”

“Don't trash where we splash”

 Selected artists will be honored in a ribbon cutting ceremony at a later date.

How to Submit Your Artistic Work

When children have completed their artistic masterpiece, they can mail or drop it off at the Municipal Services Center at 100 S. Myrtle Ave., Clearwater, FL 33765.

Participants also have the option of scanning the document and submitting it electronically below. If the art is scanned, it must be at a resolution of 300 dpi. If you need assistance or a scanner, the Clearwater Library has a free large-format scanner at the Main Library, 100 N. Osceola Ave.

Please keep a copy of your artwork in the event the city needs to follow up or re-scan the document.

By submitting your artwork, you are agreeing to allow the city to use and display your artwork in city projects.

    Learn More About Litter And How It Impacts Our Beaches
    • Never litter. Always place your trash in a garage can and your recycling in a recycling can.
    • Choose reusable items and try to avoid single-use plastic items like plastic bags, bottles, straws, and utensils
    • Volunteer in a neighborhood or beach cleanup. Sign up for one.

    More than 80 percent of the garbage that ends up in our oceans comes from our neighborhoods, parks, streets and yards. This garbage can hurt marine animals and make it less fun to spend a day at the beach.
