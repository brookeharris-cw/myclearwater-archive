The city of Clearwater Athletics Division issues permits for the use of athletic fields to non-profit organizations, schools, the general public, and for-profit entities for recreation activities, athletic tournaments, and competitive programs.

General Facility Information

General Athletic Reservation Guidelines

  • All renters must have a formal reservation receipt to use any athletic facility or amenity owned by the city of Clearwater. Reservation requests must be submitted in writing to the Athletics Division.

  • Reservation requests can be submitted via email to athletics@myclearwater.com or by completing an online Athletic Reservation Request form located at www.myclearwaterparks.com/athletics. Renters may be required to show proof of reservation. Failure to provide proof of reservation will result in the renter being unable to continue use of the facility.

  • All reservation requests must be made in advance of use. Staff will review your request within 2-5 business days to determine availability.

  • On-going, recurring reservation requests from established renters must be submitted no more than 365 days or less than fifteen (15) days prior to the first requested dates.

  • Reservations may be made in increments of 15 minutes with a minimum of 1 hour.

  • Reservation times are permitted between 8am-10:30pm. Refer to section XI for specific location restrictions.

  • Lights are scheduled for 15 minutes after reservation end time to account for clean up and safe facility exit. For specific location light restrictions, please refer to section IX.

  • Submission of a request does NOT guarantee approval.

  • The city of Clearwater reserves the right to move or cancel usage for any group due to maintenance or safety concerns, scheduling conflicts or failure to adhere to City policies and procedures, including payment of account balances within allotted time, even after a permit is issued.

  • Any scheduled usage will be charged if proper notice of cancellation or change is not provided.

  • Third Party reservations are strictly prohibited.

  • Athletic fields may only be used for the sport in which they were designed, unless otherwise approved.

  • All communications regarding reservation changes, cancellations or issues are to be made between the Athletics Supervisor and primary scheduling representative.

  • Any vendor sales (merchandise or concessions) must be approved by the city of Clearwater Athletics Division, in writing, prior to the event.

Insurance

All renters must provide a Certificate or Certificates of Insurance showing existence of coverage. The City is to be specifically listed as certificate holder and included as an additional insured on all liability coverage. The name on the Certificate of Insurance (COI) must match the team/organization name.

Insurance requirements include:

  • The renter further agrees to carry at its own cost and expense, acquire and maintain throughout all reservation dates with the City, sufficient insurance to adequately protect the respective interest of the parties. Specifically, the renter must carry the following minimum types and amounts of insurance on an occurrence basis, or in the case of coverage that cannot be obtained on an occurrence basis then coverage can be obtained on a claims-made basis with a minimum of three (3) year tail following the last date of coverage. These insurance requirements shall not limit the liability of the renter. The City does not represent that these types or amounts of insurance to be sufficient or adequate to protect the renter’s interests or liabilities, but are merely minimums:
    • Comprehensive General Liability insurance on an "occurrence" basis including but not limited to bodily injury, personal injury, and property damage with limits not less than $1,000,000 per occurrence, $2,000,000 aggregate.
      • Automobile Liability insurance for any motor vehicle owned by, hired by, used by, or used on behalf of the renter, providing Bodily Injury Liability and Property Damage Liability covering any injury or damage with limits no less than $1,000,000 per occurrence, $2,000,000 aggregate. If a renter does not own any motor vehicles or use City Property as the origin for group travel this insurance is not required.
      • The City recommends but does not require the following coverages to protect the renter. The City is not liable and shall be indemnified against any claims arising from any claims made against the recommended coverages provided below.
        • i. Property Insurance
          ii. Crime and Employee Dishonest Coverage
          iii. Sexual Abuse & Molestation Coverage
          iv. Workers Compensation Coverage
          v. Employers Liability Insurance
  • OTHER INSURANCE PROVISIONS: The General Liability and Automobile Liability policies are to contain, or be endorsed to contain, the following provisions:
    • PRIMARY INSURANCE COVERAGE: For any claims related to this Agreement, the renter’s insurance coverage shall be primary insurance as respects the City, its officers, officials, employees, and volunteers. Any insurance or self-insurance maintained by the City, its officers, officials, employees, or volunteers shall be excess of the renter’s insurance and shall not contribute to it.
    • RIGHT OF RECOVERY: Except for Workers’ Compensation, the renter waives its right of recovery against the City, to the extent permitted by its insurance policies.
  • CERTIFICATE OF INSURANCE/CERTIFIED COPIES OF POLICIES: The renter will provide the City with a Certificate or Certificates of Insurance showing the existence of coverage. In addition, the renter will provide to the City, if asked in writing, certified copies of all policies of insurance. The renter will maintain the required coverage with a current Certificate or Certificates of Insurance throughout the renter’s approved usage with the City. New certificates and new certified copies of policies shall be provided to the City whenever any policy is renewed, revised, or obtained from other insurers. Athletic Field Prioritization

The Athletics staff will attempt to accommodate all field space requests received. When there are field space allocation conflicts, staff will assign usage based upon the prioritization defined below, historical usage by an organization and organization’s conformance to rules, regulations, and recreation permit requirements. Field availability may also depend upon maintenance, and prescribed field rest periods to ensure safe playing surfaces.

1. City of Clearwater produced or sponsored events, leagues or activities
2. City of Clearwater co-sponsored youth leagues with a current Council approved annual agreement
3. Recurring, on-going “established” organization use and groups with current agreements
4. Tournaments & general renters

Billing

Any organization with a non-established pattern of use must pay all fees at the time of the reservation.

• Outlined insurance requirements are required for all types or length of usage

Any organization with an established pattern of use and conducting on-going, recurring usage of our athletic facilities will be billed for reservations on the 1st of every month for the prior month’s usage. Invoices must be paid within ten (10) business days to keep account in good standing with the city of Clearwater.

An organization may be deemed an “established” account by:

  • Keeping their account current and having no outstanding balances due
  • Having been a regular user for more than 90 days
  • Having current insurance that meets the requirements set by the city of Clearwater
  • Adhering to the policies and procedures outlined within this document

Failure to provide payment as required may result in future or current facility usage being suspended or removed.

Discrepancies with billing must be submitted for review to the city of Clearwater Athletics staff within five (5) business days.

Payments

Payments can be made by check, money order or credit card. Personal checks can only be used if paying in person with proper identification for taxable usage. Credit card payments can be made in person or online. Organizational checks are permitted via mail or in person. No cash will be accepted.

A renter will require a login and password to make online credit card payments. Please contact the Athletics Supervisor for instructions and to set up your online account.

Cancellations & Rainouts Notifications

Rainouts

  • The city of Clearwater Athletics Division will notify renters of weekday rainouts using the Rainout Line notification system.
  • • Renters can access this information after 5pm in any of the ways listed below:
    •  Call the Rainout Line at 727-268-8577 after 5 p.m.
    • Download the FREE iPhone and Android app to quickly check the field’s status after 5 p.m.
  • Sign up for email or text notifications. 
  • If the rainout line has not been updated by 5 p.m., contact the Athletics Supervisor at (727) 224-2787
  • The city will cancel light usage, if applicable, for renters if fields are closed by city staff.
  • Cancellations made by the city of Clearwater will automatically result in removal of fees on account for that date. If a payment had previously been made, a credit will be placed on account after cancellation for future usage.
  • If the city doesn’t cancel usage a renter will assume all responsibility to monitor weather conditions and evacuate playing fields of participants and spectators when the threat of lightning or dangerous conditions approach. A renter will cancel games or practices when field or playing conditions could cause harm or damage to participants or to the facilities (i.e. flooded fields).
  • Renters are required to adhere to the “30/30” rule which is as follows: When you see lightning begin counting. If you hear thunder before you reach 30 the storm is close enough to be dangerous. Clear the fields and seek shelter. Wait 30 minutes of lightning free time before resuming activities.
  •  A renter’s primary scheduling representative must contact the Athletics Supervisor by email at athletics@MyClearwater.com, via text or VM at 727-224-2787 within two (2) business days of an on-site rainout cancellation to receive a credit on account and to not be charged for scheduled usage. In addition, the renter must contact MUSCO, if applicable, directly to cancel light usage for practice or day affected, if rainout determined by user and not by City. The city reserves the right to charge the user for any light usage not cancelled.

Other Cancellations or Changes

A renter’s primary scheduling representative must contact the Athletics Supervisor by email at athletics@MyClearwater.com at least five (5) business days prior to a cancellation or reservation change to receive credit for field usage.

Field Lights
  1. Field lights will be charged to the renter in 15-minute increments based on the sunset schedule, if applicable.
  2. Most athletic facilities have automatic lights based on reservation schedules. Any facilities without automatic lights will be discussed with the renter in advance.
Keys

All organizations provided a city of Clearwater light or access key are required to return the keys at the end of their season or as requested by City staff. If keys are not returned within 10 days a $100 key fee will be assessed. Duplicating of the keys is not permitted. Any organization determined to be in violation will have any key privileges revoked.

A $20 lost key fee will be applied if an organization reports a lost key. Any replacement keys will be provided upon request and approval from the city of Clearwater once the fee has been paid.

DR14

If applicable, the renter shall provide a current DR-14 indicating tax exempt status. Failure to provide a DR-14 will result in taxes being charged accordingly.

Any form of payment used for tax exempt organizations must match the specific name as shown on the DR-14.

Acknowledgement
All athletic field renters are required to submit, annually, a signed Acknowledgement of Agreement of Terms and Understanding of Policies, Guidelines and Ordinances. (Form A)

Facility Rental Health & Safety Plan

To assist in the city of Clearwater’s continued efforts to ensure a safe and enjoyable environment for the visitors to our athletic facilities, all users must submit an event Health & Safety Plan. These plans are a way to outline the course of the days’ activities and what steps the user is taking to provide a safe and enjoyable experience for their participants and spectators. When developing these plans, users should consider the below recommendations in addition to current CDC and local guidelines. All plans will be reviewed by the Events and Athletics management team prior to confirmation of the athletic field reservations.

Rules, Requirements and Guidelines for Users
  • All users are required to submit a written event Health & Safety Plan for review and approval.
  • It is suggested that groups check with insurance company with questions or concerns regarding liability.
  • Those who feel ill should stay home.
  • Users are required to provide estimated attendance for each scheduled event/activity requested to include players, coaches, spectators, and staff.
  • Organizations should be monitoring for adherence to submitted plans. Inability to follow approved plans may result in suspension from play for a minimum of 14 days.
  • Face coverings are recommended, but not required, while in indoor common spaces or outdoors when physical distancing cannot be attained.
  • Groups are required to include a description of steps to be taken to adhere to physical distancing guidelines.
  • Suggest staggered practice and game times.
  • Players and coaches should maintain moderate physical distancing in dugouts and during practices.
  • It is recommended that organizations sanitize/wipe down common touch points between practice or games sessions.
  • Sharing of personal equipment or related items (uniforms, bats, gloves, etc.) should be avoided. If any equipment is shared, it is recommended that it is sanitized between use.
  • All visiting teams, spectators and volunteers need to be made aware of the event Health and Safety Plan and adhere to these guidelines while at the venue.
  • Organizations with concession stands must include additional details on concession set-up for safe operation and transactions.
  • Cashless concession stand operations is recommended.
  • Groups should have disposable PPE items to their first aide supply kits.
  • Updated plans are required for each new season of use or additional activity (i.e. moving use from practice to games, special events).
  • Any special event/non-traditional athletic activity requires a layout map to include entry/exit, spectator flow including any restrictions associated with spectators, physical distancing markings where it may be necessary, and vendor or any other set up locations.
  • Upon request, users may will need to provide attendance, rosters and/or line up cards for any team, staff, or official participating in their event.
  • Anyone who becomes ill should be sent home.
  • Inform anyone exposed to a person diagnosed with COVID-19 to follow CDC recommendations.
  • Please notify Carla Scrivener (Carla.Scrivener@myclearwater.com – 727-562-4839 or Kris Koch (kris.koch@myclearwater.com / 727-562-4805) if anyone in the organization tests positive for COVID-19.
