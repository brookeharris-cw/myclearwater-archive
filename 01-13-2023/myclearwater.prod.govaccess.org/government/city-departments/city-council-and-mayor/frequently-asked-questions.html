What are the services/responsibilities of the City Council?

The City Council sets policy and makes decisions concerning many different aspects of local government -- such as tax rates, annexations, property code variances and large contract awards. Clearwater's municipal administration is patterned after the Council-Manager form of government. This system of joint government combines the strong political leadership of elected officials (the City Council) with the strong management skills of a professional City Manager who prepares an annual budget for the Council's action, serves as the Council's chief advisor, recruits and hires the government's staff and sees to it that the Council's programs and policies are carried out. The Council also hires the City Attorney who is the full-time legal advisor for the city. 

The City Council also has the following responsibilities:

  • Evaluates the job performance of the City Manager and the City Attorney
  • Attends special workshops/meetings
  • Is the City liaison for various area-wide boards
  • Represents the City for numerous ribbon cuttings, opening day festivities and organizational welcomes

How many Council Members are there and how long do they serve?

The City Council is comprised of five members -- one Mayor and four Council members -- who each serve four-year terms. A person can serve two consecutive terms as a City Council member, after which he/she cannot hold any City Council seat other than Mayor for a minimum of two years. The Mayor position also has a two-term limit after which he/she cannot serve as a City Council member or Mayor for a minimum of two years.

Back to Top

How are Council Members elected ?

Members of the City Council are elected at large and elections are nonpartisan. Special elections may be called by the Council at any time.

How much are the Councilmembers paid?

The Mayor is paid $26,796 per year, and the Vice-Mayor and Councilmembers are paid $22,330.27 per year.

Back to Top

How can I become a candidate for the City Council?

To become a candidate you must be:

  • A registered voter of the city of Clearwater
  • A resident of the city for at least one continuous year
  • At least 18 years of age

For more information contact the City Clerk.

Back to Top

When does the City Council meet?

The Council holds public meetings on the first and third Thursday of each month in the City Hall Council Chambers at 6 p.m. 

To prepare for these meetings, the City Council also holds a work session on the Monday prior to each Thursday night meeting. The work sessions are at usually at 9 a.m. While the work sessions are open to the public, there is no format for public comment.

Back to Top

How can I get an agenda for a Council meeting?

Agendas for upcoming meetings are available on the City Council agendas and minutes pages, on the C-VIEW TV government access cable channel, and at most city of Clearwater buildings and facilities. Agendas are usually published a few days to a week before the meeting. C-VIEW is cablecast on Spectrum Channel 638, WOW! Channel 15 and Frontier Channel 30.

Back to Top

How can I speak at a Council meeting?

The public is permitted--and encouraged--to speak at the Thursday Council meetings. (Remember that while the public can attend the Monday work sessions, there is no provision for public comment.) Depending on your topic, there are two ways to address the Council. For topics that are on the agenda, the Council will invite citizens to speak at the appropriate time during the meeting. For topics that are not on the agenda, the Council provides time prior to beginning the agenda topics for citizens to speak on any subject relevant to the City of Clearwater. Speakers are allotted a maximum of three minutes for non-agenda topics.

Back to Top

What are the services/responsibilities of the Mayor?

Clearwater's Mayor is elected for a four-year term. The duties of the Mayor's position include presiding at all public meetings and acting as the official head of the city for service of process, by the governor for purposes of military law and for all ceremonial purposes. The Mayor has an equal vote with the other four Councilmembers and does not have any veto power.
