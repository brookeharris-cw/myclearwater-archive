City Sidewalks Get a Facelift

Ever notice a sidewalk that needs repair in your neighborhood? The city of Clearwater is on it.

A major effort is under way to repair hundreds of sidewalk issues that have been identified. That’s great news for walkers, runners, bicyclists and anyone else who uses sidewalks to get around the city.

Our Progress

Progress is happening now along the city's 365 miles of sidewalk. Residents should notice a difference in sidewalk problems soon, if they haven’t already.

“I’m committed to improving our neighborhoods,” said Jon Jennings, Clearwater’s city manager. “When I started here at the city, I noticed we had a backlog of sidewalks that are in a state of disrepair. Our teams are working hard, and they’re making significant progress, having fixed many of these backlogged requests already.”

In just a few short months, the city already has repaired many of the city’s broken sidewalks. Some repairs are easy, but others are more complicated, because of tree roots that are buckling the concrete or trees that need to be removed. The city has a plan for these tricky repairs, too.

This year alone, the city is spending an estimated $1.62 million on sidewalk improvements. Work is being done by city crews and contracted staff.

We're Creating a Database

The city is also in the beginning stages of building a program that collects 3D imagery of Clearwater’s sidewalk surfaces. It’s basically radar for pavement, scanning the ground. A sidewalk condition index is created, which helps staff prioritize which sidewalks to repair in a more strategic manner.

The goal is to speed up the process of fixing sidewalks, which ultimately will improve our neighborhoods and the safety of our walkways. The database should be complete in late 2022.

Report a Cracked Sidewalk
If you see a sidewalk that needs to be repaired, let us know about it by submitting a ticket using the street and sidewalk maintenance request.

N.E. Water Reclamation Facility Improvements

Location: 3290 State Road 580
Construction Start/Finish: June 2021 / May 2023
Construction Costs: $18.4 million

  • This project consists of modifications and improvements to preliminary and primary treatment systems, sludge handling for digestion and dewatering, and installation of a new equalization tank (EQT). 
  • Pulsed air mixing system installation began and is expected through mid-June. Hydrostatic test and prep/form for pumps expected to begin mid-June. 
  • Primary sludge pipework continues and is expected through early-June. Prep/form for Salsnes filter slab expected shortly after. 
  • Pickett thickeners structural work is complete and grit removal structures have been poured. Headcell installation began and is expected through early June. Odor control system has been partially demolished. Demolition will finish once the plant is on bypass (anticipated first week of June). 
  • Excavation and installation of underground piping for blend tanks continues and is expected through mid-June.  
  • Headworks bypass plan has been approved as noted. Meanwhile, bypass piping, pumps, taps, and grit removal are being installed and expected through end of May. Performance test on bypass expected shortly to begin first week of June.

Frequently Asked Questions about the N.E. Water Reclamation Facility Improvements

What is the Northeast Water Reclamation Facility Improvements Project, and how will it affect me?

The Northeast Water Reclamation Facility (NE WRF) Improvements Project involves upgrades and replacement of existing wastewater treatment facilities and processes, which will improve the plant's efficiency and help to reduce operating costs and impacts to the environment. The project is not expected to affect nearby residents other than by improving the efficiency of the facility that processes their wastewater.

How is the City of Safety Habor involved with the project?
The city of Safety Harbor’s wastewater is treated at the Northeast water reclamation facility and therefore is cost sharing the construction of the project with the city of Clearwater.
Where exactly is this project taking place?
Construction will be done at the Northeast Water Reclamation Facility, which is located north of State Road 580 and just east of McMullen Booth Road, adjacent to the Chi Chi Rodriguez golf range.
What does the project involve?
The Northeast WRF Improvement Project consists of the installation of a new flow equalization tank, new filters for primary treatment, a new grit removal system, new sludge blending tanks, and other associated work.
When will construction be taking place?
Construction began in June 2021, and the project is expected to be completed in 2023. The contractor will work between the hours of 7 a.m. to 7 p.m., Monday through Friday.
How will traffic be handled during construction?
The city does not expect residents to be affected by traffic impacts. The construction will be inside of the wastewater treatment plant; there will not be any construction in public roadways. However, you may see more city vehicles going in and out of the plant, which will increase due to construction workers and deliveries of materials and equipment. 
What about school buses and public transportation?
Public transportation and school bus routes will not be affected.
What construction would occur at night or on weekends?
The contractor’s normal working hours are Monday through Friday between 7 a.m. and 7 p.m. Night and weekend work will be rare. 
My property line is bordering the treatment plant. What can I expect during construction and when it’s finished?
You may hear noise from heavy equipment during active construction hours. There is no construction occurring within 500 to 1,000 feet from residents’ property lines. A new equalization tank will replace an existing tank; the new tank is about the same height as the existing control building that is located in the approximate center of the site.
Will the treatment plant be put out of service during construction?
The treatment plant will remain in operation throughout construction.
Will my utilities be affected during construction?
Residents’ utilities will not be impacted by this project.
How much does the project cost?
The Northeast Water Reclamation Facility Improvement project’s construction cost is $18.4 million.
Will this project increase my utility bill?
No, your utility bill will not go up due to this project. Funds for this project have already been set aside.
What company is doing the construction work?
Poole & Kent Company of Florida is the construction contractor for this project.
